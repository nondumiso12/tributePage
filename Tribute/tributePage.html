Winnie Madikizela Mandela

Mother of nation(South African anti-apartheid activist)

Winnie Madikizela-Mandela OLS MP,a South African anti-apartheid activest and politician and ex-wife of Nelson Mandela.

Here's a timeline of Winnie Madikizela Mandeni's life:

  • 26 September 1936-Born in Bizana,Eastern Cape.
  • 1953-She moves to Johannesburg after being admitted at the Jan Hofmeyr School for Social Work.
  • 1958-Madikizela-Mandela marries Nelson Mandela in Bizana in the Eastern Cape.
  • 1959-She gives birth to her first child,Zenani Mandela.
  • 1960-Madikizela-Mandela gives birth to her second daughter,Zandziswa.
  • 1962-Madikizela-Mandela is banned by the apartheid government and her movements restricted to the magisterial district of Johannesburg.The order also stipulated that she is not permitted to be quoted in the media.This follows the arrest of her husband during the previous year.
  • 1965-A severe banning order is handed to Winnie,limiting her movements and banning her from moving anywhere other than her neighbourhood of Orlando West.
  • 1969-Winnie's home is raided.She is arrested and kept in solitary confinement for 17 months.
  • 1973-She is arrested again for meeting with another banned person,a photographer from Drum magazine,Peter Magubane.She is handed a 12-month sentence.
  • 1977-Known as the year of the Brandfort banishment,Winnie is sent to the small town 400kilometres away from Johannesburg for 8years.
  • 1986-She returns to her home at 8115 Vilakazi Street,Orlando West in Soweto.
  • 1990-Winnie is present when Nelson Mandela is released from Victor Verster Prison.
  • 1992-She becomes President of the ANC Women's League(ANCWL).
  • 1994-She is appointed Deputy Minister of Arts,Culture,Science and Technology,but is dismissed the following year for allegations of financial mismanagement.
  • 2016-Celebrates her 80th birthday at a gala at Cape Town's Mount Nelson Hotel with family,friends and colleagues.
  • 02 April 2018-Winnie Madikizela-Mandela passed away at Milpark Hospital,Johannesburg.
